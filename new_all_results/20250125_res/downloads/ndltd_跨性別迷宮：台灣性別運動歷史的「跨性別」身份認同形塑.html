本文探究「跨性別」作為身分認同，如何在台灣性別運動的歷史當中形成穩定性的概念。首先採用傅柯系譜學的觀點進行歷史研究法的分析，針對台灣八〇年代的變性醫療發展與九〇年代的性別運動倡議這兩段歷史過程進行處理，從中解構當代「跨性別」身份認同如今被理所當然地視為本質性的存在；並且，以全球在地化的視角，提出主體之於「跨性別」身份認同形塑過程的能動性與重要性，從主體認同實踐歷程重新思考認同政治下的身份範疇對於個別主體與跨性別社群所帶來的影響。奠基於上述的討論，本文從歷史論述的考究來聚焦「跨性別」身份認同邊界的成形，並以主體認同實踐的歷程進一步探討主體與認同邊界之間的互動與接合關係，書寫出性別運動的歷史如何影響了當代「跨性別」身份認同形成穩定性的概念。

This study explores how “transgender” as an identity has become an established concept throughout the history of gender movement in Taiwan. First, an analysis based on the historical method that adopts Foucault’s genealogical approach is conducted to review the historical process of both the medical development in gender reassignment procedures in the 80s and the gender movement in the 90s in Taiwan, and hence, to deconstruct how the identity of “transgender” came to be regarded as an intrinsic existence. Meanwhile, from a glocalizational point of view, this study points out the agency and importance of subjects in the process of shaping the transgender identity, and furthermore, reflects the impact of identity category in the sense of identity politics on individual subjects and the transgender community by reviewing subjects’ journey of practicing their identity.Departing from the aforementioned discussions, this study focuses on how the boundary of transgender identity is formed by reviewing historical narratives, and further examines the interaction as well as linkage between subjects and their identity boundary based on subjects’ journey of identity practice. This study aims to portray how the history of gender movement has impacted the process of the transgender identity growing into an established concept.
