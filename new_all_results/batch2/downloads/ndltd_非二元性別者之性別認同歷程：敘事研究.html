本研究採質性研究敘事分析，透過訪談五位非二元性別者，深入探討他們的性別認同歷程與出櫃經驗，以及非二元性別認同在生命中的意義。本研究採用社會建構論的觀點作為分析基礎，以理解非二元性別者富流動性的認同歷程。研究結果發現，非二元性別者大多在年輕時便對出生指定性別與相對應的社會性別規範產生不適感，在獲取性別認同資訊與建立認同後，持續探索認同、調整性別表現，以實踐自己的性別認同。非二元性別者現身／出櫃時，需面對社會對非二元性別所知甚少的困境，以及反覆解釋自身認同的情緒勞動，因而採取策略性現身／出櫃。非二元性別認同讓參與者打破性別二分的框架，找到自我定位，並將此轉化為自我賦權及推動社會變革的動力。本研究突顯了在台灣文化背景下，非二元性別者所經歷的特殊情境，包含就讀單一性別學校對認同的影響、過往獲取性別認同資訊門檻較高。本研究填補了台灣在非二元性別研究領域的空白，並對心理諮商實務提出具體建議，旨在增強對非二元性別者的理解與支持。
This study is aimed to explore the gender identity process and coming out experiences and delve into the significance of non-binary identity of five individuals in their lives. Grounded in the perspective of social construction-ism, this research reveals the fluid identity process of non-binary individuals by means of qualitative research with narrative analysis. The findings have in-dicated that most non-binary individuals experienced discomfort with their as-signed gender and its related social norms at young age. After ascertaining and establishing their gender identity, they continued to explore and adjust their gender expression to actualize their gender identity. When exposing their non-binary identity or coming out, they faced societal challenges due to limited public awareness about non-binary gender and the emotional labor involved in repeatedly explaining their identity, leading to strategic decisions about coming out. Furthermore, embracing a non-binary identity allowed the partic-ipants to break free from the binary gender framework, find self-positioning, and transform this recognition into empowerment for themselves and as a cat-alyst for societal change. Also, this study highlights the unique circumstances non-binary individuals experience in the Taiwanese cultural context, including the impact of attending single-gender schools and the higher barriers to ac-cessing gender identity information. It thus fills a gap in non-binary gender research in Taiwan and offers concrete suggestions for counseling practices, aiming to enhance understanding and support for non-binary individuals.
