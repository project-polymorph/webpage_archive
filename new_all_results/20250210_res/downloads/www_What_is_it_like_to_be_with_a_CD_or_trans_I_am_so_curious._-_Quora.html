I say this as a trans woman who is still very new to being trans, though who has had a fair amount of experience presenting feminine. I am 5′11″ tall, heavy build, thinning hair and 45 years of experience as a man. I have been gendered properly on occasion but not often. These are the things that I am working on to improve my passability in public.

I think that passing is a combination of many aspects of presentation. The first two I have tackled are my voice and my facial hair. If you speak with a low male voice it is a dead giveaway, Cis women generally don't talk like that. Facial hair is another giveaway. It is really obvious as women don't have 5 o'clock shadow. Despite shaving beard shadow is hard to truly conceal and it takes only seconds to get clocked.

Hairline or lack of hair in another one. If your hair starts high up on your forehead, is receding or you have a bald or thin spot, it is a giveaway. I know that women can have hair loss too but the problem as a trans woman is that we don't have many other feminine features to cover for that. I struggle with this aspect of my appearance and I am certainly going to look into hair transplant. I am also highly considering a wig or something else to help hide my bald area. My hair is long and from certain angles looks feminine, but the forest is more like thin grassland on top and gives me away.

Mannerisms play a large part. How you walk, sit, stand and otherwise hold your body will give you away. This is hard to figure out because it requires a lot of mental effort to understand what feminine mannerisms are and how to reproduce them not to mention doing it consistently.

Beer gut. If your gut is hanging over your wasteband it is obvious that you are a guy. I have this problem. As I lose weight this will become less obvious and weight distribution on hormones takes a long time. Losing weight is hard. I have struggled with it for years. I have made progress in the past and know what the results look like and I know that it will help.

Clothing. Dressing in clothing that expresses femininity really helps. Again if you are a beautiful woman you are still beautiful no matter what you wear. As a trans woman you have fewer feminine features to fall back on. Clothing fit and style should be recognizably feminine and your other characteristics that you work on will compliment it.

Confidence. Being confident in yourself helps others see who you are. If you are nervous then you will appear as though you are hiding something or you will make people feel uncomfortable around you. If you are confident with your head held high people are far less likely to question the rest of your presentation. By the way this works for many aspects of how others see you in life. As someone who has crossdressed for many years in public even while ignoring the majority of what I just said, my confidence breaks down barriers and shuts down stares, gawking and many other reactions to me. If I appear as though things are normal it puts others at ease and despite standing out, they are far less likely to question my legitimacy.

Doing all these things is a huge amount of work. It is not for everyone and is definitely not required to be valid as a trans woman. For me it is part of my journey and I will continue to work on it. Confidence in myself and seeing the transformation of others tells me that I will get there…one day.
