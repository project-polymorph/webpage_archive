本研究以系統的觀點，探討兩個議題：1.跨性別者的性別認同歷程以及家人對此的理解與應對；2.現身前後與家人互動的變化過程。
本研究採用敘事研究法及對偶研究法，訪談了三個跨性別者家庭，意即跨性別者及其一名家人。研究參與者的敘說內容包含個人的性別認同探索歷程、家人的理解與應對以及跨性別者在現身前後與家庭成員互動的變化歷程。方法訪談進行完畢將謄寫成逐字稿，反覆閱讀，並且以「整體-內容」作為分析方法，並以家庭互動的內涵，分析他們的互動型態、關係狀態、互動轉變因素等。
本研究結果如下：跨性別者在性別認同歷程中因為缺乏友善的資訊而在探索過程感到混淆與痛苦，甚至確認身分以後的現身過程也很反覆與複雜，家人其實難以理解。跨性別者在自我轉變後的打扮、使用荷爾蒙等，他們仍得面對自我接納、重新學習新的性別生活、選擇與過去社交網絡切割等的議題。在這過程中，跨性別者也受憂鬱症與自殺議題所苦，自我接納是需要持續處理的議題。性別認同階段是同時並行與持續發展，而跨性別者需要重要他人的認同與支持來建立自我認同。家人方面，他們理解跨性別者的方式是漸進式的，而且父親與母親的應對方式，較分為情緒截斷與情緒涉入。跨性別者因身分別的不同，家人的應對方式各有差異，而且這反映了他們複雜的情緒，如恐懼感。家人的應對策略也從僵化到彈性，甚至情緒也貫穿整個調適歷程。
互動方面，跨性別者與家人衝突的開始來自於他們的陰柔特質、打扮及現身。他們彼此複雜的情緒狀態使關係持續對立與拉扯，而家人的強烈反對或者迴避的態度，也改變彼此的互動。此外，當家人擔心跨性別者的安危時，他們的對立關係開始改變。這過程也會面臨不少衝突，當中的協商與溝通策略也影響互動的結果，手足的需要也在此過程中被父母忽略。新的家庭結構與結盟關係的建立，也為關係帶來正向的改變並強化支持系統。接納期時，他們彼此處理零星衝突的方式已顯不同，家人對跨性別者的支持與接納來自於看見他的成長與改變，而跨性別者也因家人的理解與同理改變了他與家人的互動模式。
本研究也發現，跨性別者與家人互動的循環歷程具有五個特性：1.貫穿性；2.反覆性；3.延續性；3.變動性；4.循環性。綜合上述的討論，本研究除了統整並討論上述研究發現外，也進一步對未來研究方向及諮商實務工作者提出建議，並且再對研究歷程進行反思。



This study has two major purposes: (1) to explore the process of transgender’s gender identity and their families’ comprehension and responses; (2) to explore the changing process of transgender and families’ interaction in their coming-out period.
Narrative method and dyadic methods were applied in this study. Three families’ data were collected in dyadic method. This study’s participants were included two transgenders with their mothers, and a transgender with his sister. The data were analyzed based on the “holistic-content” approach.
The results were shown: Firstly, lack of friendly information in the gender identity exploration stage brought them more suffering and confused to confirm who they are. The coming out process of transgender was complicated and repeated. The self-acceptance issue, beginning of the new gender life and the passing process always had been the continued issues after the transition process. By the way, transgender always suffers in the depression and suicide issue, self-acceptance still was the main issue in spite of passing to a new identity. The gender identity development stages were a continuous and concurrent process, significant others’ acceptance and supports were important to them. On the other hand, families went through a suffering and confrontation process till they could comprehend what were transgender’s experiences and needs. Father and mother’s coping styles were different. The families’ responses were reflected the fears and worries toward the transgender's identity. The coping styles of family were changing from rigid to more flexible. The emotions and the feelings of families were complicated and run through the adjustment process.
Secondly, the process of interaction between transgender and families became more conflicts and rigid during their coming out. Each family member’s emotion and stress made their interaction more antagonistic. Two patterns of responses included confrontation and avoidance were changed the interaction of them in different condition. The tension between transgender and the families had eased up when families were worried about the transgender’s self-safety. Besides that, sibling’s needs always had been neglected by parents in this process. Conflicts were never ending up while some actions had triggered off the emotions of each other. The negotiations and communication skills had always been applied. Then, the forming of new family structure, families’ supports and the formation of an alliance between transgender and families were brought them to the positive interactions.
The gender identity process and the interaction between transgender and families were shown in five characteristics: throughout, repetitively, continuously, changeably and circularity.
In conclusion, the emotions and reactions which underlay in the gender identity process of transgender were not the intrinsic development only, whereas there were the results of the interaction with social context, included families, social values and so on. This study also showed the interaction process between transgender and families with more detailedly and dynamically. Finally, based on the aforementioned, the recommendations for future studies, counseling practitioner were served.
